# Product identity

One guiding principle: **it's hilariously fast.** Everything else — the
voice, the jokes, the design decisions — flows from that. Fast enough to be
funny about it. The terminal they tell you not to worry about.

`bwn` is the character — short, lowercase, terminal-native; it's what humans
type and what prose calls the tool. `buildwithnexus` is the formal wordmark:
package registries, page titles, legal.

## The personality

Quick, confident, funny in passing. bwn is the tool that finished before you
looked up, and its humor comes from that speed — never from mocking the user,
never from filler. Think: a one-liner while the tests run, not a comedy set
before they start.

Where the jokes live:

- **Startup tips** — one rotating dim line under the banner: half real tips,
  half quips. This is the personality's home turf.
- **Release notes and site copy** — one wink per surface ("hilariously
  fast", "deliberately boring launcher"). Understatement over exclamation.
- **Never in error paths.** An error message's only job is the next step:
  what happened, the exact command that recovers. Jokes in errors read as
  mockery when someone's stuck. Errors stay serious; that contrast is what
  makes the rest land.

## Rules that keep it honest

1. **Funny ≠ fake.** Claims still carry numbers ("966µs → 4.5µs — we timed
   it so you don't have to feel it"). A joke built on a real measurement is
   the brand; a joke built on hype is the anti-brand.
2. **Speed is the substance, humor is the delivery.** If a release doesn't
   make something faster, smaller, or more instant, the copy doesn't get to
   swagger about it.
3. **Errors teach, tips entertain.** Hard boundary.
4. **Punch at the problem, never the user.** "it can take it" (about the
   agent) — yes. Anything that makes the user feel slow — no.
5. **No competitor names in shipped copy.** The meme works better anonymous:
   "the other terminals keep asking about bwn."
6. **lowercase `bwn` in prose and examples**; `buildwithnexus` for the
   wordmark and registries.

## Register boundary

The `letsbeheroes` skill pack speaks in its own earnest register ("the
oath"); that stays inside the skill pack. bwn's own voice is the fast one
with the dry grin.
